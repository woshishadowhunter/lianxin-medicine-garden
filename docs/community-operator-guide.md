# Community Operator Guide

This guide is for community workers, school staff, and public-welfare organizers who want to understand whether Lianxin Medicine Garden fits their activity.

## Best-fit Scenarios

Use this project when your activity needs:

- Family or participant binding.
- Photo-backed daily submissions.
- Administrator review before records count.
- Long-running participation tracking.
- Exportable data for semester, annual, or community reports.
- A WeChat Mini Program experience rather than a general web form.

## Operating Model

1. The organizer prepares family codes and participant instructions.
2. Families bind their code in the Mini Program.
3. Families submit care records with photos.
4. Administrators review records and correct invalid submissions.
5. Participation, care frequency, and photo evidence remain available for summaries.
6. Reports can be exported for activity review.

## What You Need Before Deployment

- A WeChat Mini Program AppID.
- A WeChat Cloud Development environment.
- A person who can deploy cloud functions.
- A clear policy for administrator passwords and database permissions.
- A privacy notice for families if real photos or contact details are collected.

## Privacy Checklist

- Avoid collecting unnecessary personal data.
- Do not put real phone numbers or family names into public demo data.
- Keep administrator credentials outside Git.
- Review who can read cloud database collections.
- Decide how long photos and activity records should be retained.

## Customization Ideas

- Replace herbs with school-specific plants or activity tasks.
- Add semester report templates.
- Add synthetic demo data for training administrators.
- Add bilingual instructions for families.
